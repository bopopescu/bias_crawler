<p>A rally is planned for this weekend in Chicago's Boystown neighborhood to protest the move by the <a href="/topic/politics-government/donald-trump-PEBSL000163-topic.html" id="PEBSL000163" title="Donald Trump">Trump</a> administration to roll back federal protections for transgender students in public schools.</p><p>Close to 400 people indicated on Facebook on Thursday that they planned to attend the Stand Up for Transgender Rights event at noon Saturday at the corner of Halsted and Roscoe streets to show their support for transgender equality.</p><div class="trb_gptAd trb_ar_main_ad" data-adloader-adtype="articlebottomcube" data-adloader-label="" data-adloader-position="articleCube" data-adloader-size="300x250" data-role="adloader adloader_ad delayload delayload_done" data-state="adloader_unloaded" data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&amp;topOffset=1000000"></div>
<p>"I'm passionate about this cause," said rally organizer Kevin Morrison, 27, who is gay and has friends who identify as transgender. </p><p>On Wednesday, Secretary of Education <a href="/topic/politics-government/betsy-devos-PEGPF00208-topic.html" id="PEGPF00208" title="Betsy DeVos">Betsy DeVos</a> and Attorney General Jeff Sessions announced that the administration was rescinding a federal directive put in place by President <a href="/topic/politics-government/government/barack-obama-PEPLT007408-topic.html" id="PEPLT007408" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> that allowed transgender students to use the bathroom and locker room that matched their gender identity. The new administration's move was condemned by <b> </b> advocates for civil and lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender rights but lauded by those who viewed Obama's policy as federal overreach.</p><div class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"></div>

"What it will end up furthering is discrimination against an already-disenfranchised community. We need to show the current administration this is not how we move this country forward.".</p><p>Morrison, who  lives in Elk Grove Village, said he is in contact with Lakeview Ald. Tom Tunney's office and has talked to the 7-Eleven owner about use of the parking lot at the intersection where the rally is planned. A vigil was held at that intersection last<b> </b>June<b> </b>for victims of the Pulse nightclub shooting in Orlando, Fla. He said he has reached out to LGBT organizations around the city including the Center on Halsted as well as other minority groups to line up speakers for the event  and to create a more inclusive platform.</p><p>After a stint working on the Hillary Clinton presidential campaign in Ohio, Morrison said he has attended Donald Trump protests in Chicago including the Not My Presidents Day protest Monday near Trump Tower and a protest at O'Hare International Airport last month in the wake of Trump's  executive order banning entry of immigrants from seven majority-Muslim countries.</p><p>"We need to fight for all Americans, and we definitely aren't seeing that in this administration," Morrison said. "Any community that is going to be affected by this administration is going to be someone I'm going to stand up for."</p><div class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"></div>
